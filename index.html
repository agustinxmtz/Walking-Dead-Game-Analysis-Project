The Walking Dead

Game Analysis: The Walking Dead

About

The Walking dead is a choice driven game with consequences regarding your actions. The games philosophy ecourages you to make a choice and stick with it reaping the good or bad consequences. Depending on your choices the stories characters begin to paint an image about you. The story is very character driven, given the choice to kill, save, be-friend different characters.

Developers

The Walking dead is an episodic point-and-click horror adventure, focusing on problem solving rather than combat. The game was developed and published by Telltale Games, released in spring of 2012. With over 50 million episodes sold the game quickly became a commercial success.

In each episode, the player controls a main character as the story unfolds through various scenes. Players can explore the environment, examine items, and engage in conversations with non-player characters, choosing from several response options, including the choice to remain silent. Some scenes feature quick time events, where players must press specific buttons to respond to on-screen prompts. Failing to do so in time may result in the character's death or an undesirable outcome, causing the game to restart from the previous scene.

Why this Game?

The Game inspires players to make difficult decision and put your self in this world. Playing as Lee you get a unique perspective on the character and are given difficult choices that prompt you to think ethically or not. Its exhilarating and stressful being able to make time based decision on the fly with consequences happening real time. And of course the story is is fantastic and engaging!

Battle System

Battles are timed and straightforward, requiring players to press a specific button during action sequences. What makes them unique is that you can choose whether to kill a character or spare them. Check out the video below to see how a battle unfolds and the choices you can make while fighting.

The game excels at forcing players to make tough decisions on the spot while also allowing moments to pause and reflect. It encourages you to see things from Lee's perspective, considering various circumstances. For example, when deciding whether to spare or kill Andy, players have real agency in shaping the outcome.
"In games, choices with these components(Awareness, Gameplay consequences, Reminders, and Permanence) will evoke a strong emotional reaction, something that players discuss and think about and remember. By making choices meaningful, we help to make the game itself meaningful"

Emotional connection to the characters

The game connects deeply with players through its strong characters. Players form bonds with figures like Lee and Clementine, sharing their successes and struggles. This emotional depth makes choices feel significant and impactful. The game’s storytelling and character development pull players into an immersive experience where their decisions truly affect the story.

The characters’ growth in essential survival skills and tough decision-making is what makes this game unique. As they evolve, players form a deep emotional connection, becoming invested in their journeys.

The game offers players choices that feel impactful, as you can see how other characters react in real time. It encourages you to think about the moral implications of your decisions, whether you're taking lives or saving them. The post-apocalyptic setting adds complexity, as characters face tough situations. While zombies are the main antagonists, you soon realize that people can be even worse than the undead. As the story unfolds, it shifts to focus on the relationship between Lee and Clementine, with Lee’s main goal being to protect her. This deep connection makes players want to see the story through to the end.

Conclusion

The Walking Dead resonates with me on a deeper level because I have a daughter the same age as Clementine. As I play, I feel a fatherly instinct to protect her, and my decisions reflect what I would do if she were my own daughter. This perspective adds an extra layer of enjoyment to the game, making the story even more compelling as I want to protect, love, and understand all the unique characters.
